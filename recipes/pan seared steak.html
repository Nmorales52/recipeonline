Pan Seared Steak

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs Steak cut of choice (2 steaks)
  • 1/2 tablespoon vegtable oil, or any high heat cooking oil like canola or extra light olive oil
  • 1 1/2 teaspoon sea salt
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper, freshly ground
  • 2 tablespoon unsalted butter
  • 2 cloves garlic, peeled and quartered
  • 1 sprig fresh rosemary

Description

This Pan-Seared Steak has a garlic rosemary-infused butter that makes it taste steakhouse quality. You’ll be impressed at how easy it is to make the perfect steak – seared and caramelized on the outside, and so juicy inside.

Recipe credits:

  • Natasha's Kitchen - Blog

Steps

  1. Thoroughly pat steak dry with paper towels. Just before cooking, generously season with 1 1/2 tsp salt and 1 tsp black pepper.
  2. Heat the cast iron pan until hot then add 1/2 Tbsp oil over medium-high heat, swirling to coat. Once the oil is very hot, add steaks to the skillet. Sear the steaks on the first side for 4 minutes until a brown crust has formed then flip and cook another 3-4 minutes. Using tongs, turn the steak on its sides to render the white fat and sear the edges (1-minute per edge).
  3. Transfer steak to a cutting board, loosely cover and rest 10 minutes before slicing into 1/2" strips to serve. Spoon extra butter sauce over sliced steak to serve.
  4. Reduce heat to medium and immediately add 2 Tbsp butter, quartered garlic cloves and rosemary to the pan. Spoon the butter sauce over the steak, tilting the pan to get butter on your spoon. Continue spooning the sauce over the steak for a minute or until the steak is about 5-10 degrees from your desired doneness (the temperature will continue to rise another 5-10 degrees while steaks rest).
  5. Serve and Enjoy!

Recipe Notes

  1. Medium Rare: 145 degrees F
  2. Medium Done: 160 degrees F
  3. Well Done: 170 degrees F
